About Me

As a clinical data researcher, my ultimate goal is to aid in the progress for a healthier world by visualizing the development of clinical and nutritional trends. My work is centered around analyzing data sets related to various microbiological aspects such as microbes, viruses, and bacteria - and then mapping out the trends in these areas over time. One of the key challenges that we face in the field of microbiological research is the lack of understanding that exists around the importance of tracking and monitoring these trends. I use data science technology to aid healthcare by collecting, analyzing, and interpreting large amounts of data related to clinical and nutritional trends within an IT system. One of the primary tools I use to visualize microbiological data is Tableau. Tableau is a powerful data visualization tool that allows me to create interactive and dynamic visualizations that help me identify patterns and trends in the data. With Tableau, I can create customized dashboards that display key metrics and insights in real-time, making it easier for healthcare professionals to make informed decisions based on the latest information. By using Tableau to visualize microbiological data, I can help identify potential health risks and develop strategies to mitigate them, ultimately aiding in the progress towards a healthier world. My collaborative research and findings have been published on The American Journal of Public Health and Medium.
